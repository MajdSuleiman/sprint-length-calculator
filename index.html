Criteria 1

Business needs change a lot

This criterion evaluates the frequency and extent of changes to the business requirements during the project lifecycle.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: Limited changes with minimal impact.
  • 2-3: Moderate changes with occasional updates.
  • 4-5: Frequent changes requiring constant adjustments and flexibility from the development team.

Criteria 2

Client / Product manager needs usable increments on quick pace

How important is it to deliver small, functional parts of the product quickly to maintain client satisfaction or meet project timelines?

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: Minimal urgency for quick, usable increments.
  • 2-3: Moderate need for increments and reviews.
  • 4-5: High impact relies on frequent, usable increments for client satisfaction.

Criteria 3

Constant Need for feedback from client / product manager

This assesses how frequently feedback is required from the client or product manager during the development process.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: Feedback is rarely needed; the project can proceed with minimal input from the client or product manager.
  • 2-3: Occasional feedback is necessary to ensure alignment, but it's not a constant requirement.
  • 4-5: Continuous feedback throughout the project helps validate and align with client expectations.

Criteria 4

Product is known to us

This evaluates how familiar the team is with the product being developed.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: The product is completely new, and the team has no prior knowledge or experience with it.
  • 2-3: The team has limited familiarity with the product or has worked on similar products before.
  • 4-5: The team has a deep understanding and significant prior experience with the product, making development easier and faster.

Criteria 5

Team is new to organization/business

This evaluates how familiar the team members are with the organization or business domain.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: The team has extensive experience and is fully integrated into the organization, understanding its processes and domain deeply.
  • 2-3: The team has some exposure to the organization or business domain but may still require guidance and onboarding.
  • 4-5: The team is entirely new to the organization and unfamiliar with its business processes or domain.

Criteria 6

Project/Product has Good CI/CD

This criterion evaluates the level of automation, efficiency, and reliability in the project's Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD) pipelines. A robust CI/CD setup ensures frequent integration, quick feedback loops, and seamless deployment processes, which significantly enhance development speed and quality.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: Manual or missing CI/CD leads to inefficiencies.
  • 2-3: CI/CD is partially automated with some manual steps.
  • 4-5: Fully automated CI/CD in place, allowing frequent integration, testing, and deployment.

Criteria 7

Project/Product has LOW dependency on external parties

This criterion measures the extent to which the project relies on third-party services, vendors, or collaborators. Lower dependency on external parties enables smoother workflows, fewer delays, and greater control over the development process.

Scoring Guidelines:

  • 1: High dependency, frequent external delays.
  • 2-3: Moderate dependency, manageable delays.
  • 4-5: Minimal dependency, autonomous work without significant interruptions.
